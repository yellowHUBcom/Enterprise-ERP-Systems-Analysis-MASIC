# Technical Requirements Gathering Blueprint: Operational IS Assessment
**Target Organization:** MASIC (Mohammed I. Alsubaie & Sons Investment Company)  
**Key Stakeholder Interviewed:** Mr. Hessian (IT Management Team)  
**Lead Systems Analyst:** Lama Abdullah Al Draim  

---

## 📌 Context & Purpose
This document outlines the qualitative technical interview framework deployed during the field research phase at MASIC. The primary objective was to investigate the structural orchestration of enterprise-level Information Systems (IS), evaluate their transactional performance, and understand the core decision-making parameters governing their hybrid software stack (Microsoft Dynamics ERP).

---

## 📑 Detailed Consultation Questionnaire

### Q1: Corporate Foundation & Structural Footprint
> *"Thank you for your time Mr. Hessian. To establish an architectural baseline, could you provide a brief introduction about your organization’s operational heritage, asset classes, and when it was originally founded?"*

### Q2: Enterprise System Typology & Classification
> *"What specific classification or type of Information System is currently driving your day-to-day enterprise operations? Are there specific industry modules deployed?"*

### Q3: Architectural Advantages & Operational ROI
> *"From a systems engineering perspective, what are the primary performance advantages, scalability benefits, and operational returns on investment (ROI) that this current system delivers to MASIC?"*

### Q4: Systemic Bottlenecks & Optimization Vectors
> *"Are there any legacy technical debts, synchronization bottlenecks, or cross-platform operational issues within your current system topology that the IT team is looking to mitigate or solve?"*

### Q5: Procurement Strategy: Commercial Off-the-Shelf (COTS) vs. Custom-Built
> *"Given the specialized nature of multi-asset investment firms, would you recommend purchasing Commercial Off-the-Shelf software or building a proprietary, custom-coded information system from scratch? What are the underlying architectural or financial reasons for your stance?"*

### Q6: Business Requirements & Process Mapping
> *"What are the critical, high-priority business needs, operational workflows, and data pipelines that your information systems must sustain to align with MASIC's corporate strategy?"*

### Q7: Technical Implementation Barriers & Constraints
> *"What were the primary technical factors, infrastructure limitations, and data migration complexities that your organization encountered during the deployment or system integration phase?"*

### Q8: Problem Resolution & Algorithmic Mitigation
> *"How has the active implementation of this enterprise system directly solved legacy business constraints, reduced data isolation, or optimized manual operational overhead compared to past frameworks?"*

### Q9: Stakeholder Demographics & Technical Demand
> *"Who are your primary clients and stakeholders (e.g., family office portfolios, institutional partners), and how do their specific transactional needs influence your software features and data structures?"*

### Q10: Lifecycle Maintenance & Integrity Protocols
> *"What strategies, patch deployment protocols, and architectural reviews does the IT department enforce to keep the systems continuously updated, structurally sound, and aligned with volatile security standards?"*

### Q11: Technical Leadership & Career Vectoring
> *"Reflecting on your journey, how has managing complex enterprise systems, technical deployments, and IT infrastructure transformation influenced or accelerated your professional career trajectory?"*

### Q12: Strategic Advisory for Startups & Emerging Firms
> *"Finally, what technical or strategic advice can you share with newly founded startups or small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs) currently struggling to scale their internal information system architectures or manage early technological debt?"*
